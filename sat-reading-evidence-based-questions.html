How to Ace SAT Evidence-Based Questions

Evidence-based questions ask you to find the specific part of a passage that supports an answer. They're asking: 'prove it.' The trick is that the best evidence is almost always a direct statement, not an inference.

Your score: 0/0
Question 1

Which type of evidence is strongest for supporting claims?

Explanation: Direct statements are always stronger evidence.
Question 2

When paired with a previous question, what should you do FIRST?

Explanation: Answer main question first, then find supporting evidence.
Question 3

Best evidence that urban parks reduce stress?

Explanation: Specific data directly supports the claim.
Question 4

If no evidence options match your answer perfectly, you should:

Explanation: Evidence options can help you correct a wrong first answer.
Question 5

Common trap in evidence questions?

Explanation: The SAT loves true-but-irrelevant evidence.
